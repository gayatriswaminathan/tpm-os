# GTM Strategy

> **Status: planned.** The spec below is drafted; implementation is coming. Have a use case? Open an issue.

Turn "we're shipping a thing" into a structured, repeatable go-to-market plan.

## When to use this skill

- Planning the launch of a feature, product, or program
- Replacing ad-hoc launches with a repeatable framework
- Aligning product, marketing, and delivery on one launch narrative

## Inputs to ask for

1. What's launching and who it's for.
2. The core value proposition and the problem it solves.
3. Launch tier (quiet rollout vs. major moment) and timeline.

## Output (planned)

A go-to-market plan covering target audience and segments, positioning and messaging, channels and sequencing, success metrics, and a launch-day checklist with owners.

## Operating principles (planned)

- Start from the customer's problem, not the feature.
- Match the launch effort to the launch's actual significance.
- Define how you'll measure success before launch day, not after.
